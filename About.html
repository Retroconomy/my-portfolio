Hi there!

I’m Megan Campbell, a game design student living in the heart of Dundee—Scotland’s vibrant gaming hub. My journey into game development might be a little unconventional, but that’s what makes it so exciting! I transitioned from managing a bar to diving headfirst into the world of game design, fueled by a passion I discovered during the lockdown. What started as casual curiosity about programming and QA testing quickly became an obsession with understanding how games work, why they captivate us, and yes—how to fix those pesky bugs.

One of my favorite games of all time is Fable 3. Sure, it’s not perfect, but there’s something magical about its world, its quirky characters, and the choices it lets you make. It’s a game that taught me that even flaws can be part of what makes something memorable—a lesson I carry with me in my own projects.

I grew up in Inverness, nestled in the heart of the Highlands, where life was quieter and the scenery was breathtaking. Moving to Dundee for university has been an adjustment—city life is vibrant, exciting, and sometimes overwhelming—but it’s a challenge I’ve embraced. Every day feels like a new adventure, and I’m slowly finding my rhythm in this bustling environment.

Studying at Abertay University has been a journey of growth and discovery. Every semester brings new opportunities to test my creativity and push my limits. Once I’ve completed my coursework, I take the time to update this portfolio with fully detailed projects so you can see my progress and achievements unfold.

Whether it’s building immersive worlds, designing compelling mechanics, or solving tricky design problems, I’m all about creating experiences that stick with players long after the credits roll. So, take a look around, check out my projects, and feel free to reach out if you’d like to chat or collaborate.

Thanks for stopping by!
