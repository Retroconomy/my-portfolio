Bug Report Example 1: Infinite Oxygen Bug

Details:

Title: Infinite Oxygen Bug Occurs walking through portal with Prawn Suit.

Priority: High

Severity: Critical

Description:

Players experience infinite oxygen after exiting/entering the Prawn Suit. The oxygen meter freezes at maximum, giving players the ability to stay underwater indefinitely without the need to resurface. The only way to reset the bug is to quit your game and log back in.

Steps to Reproduce:

  1. Launch Subnautica.
  2. Load a save file where the Prawn Suit is available.
  3. Walk to the portal with the Prawn Suit on.
  4. Exit the vehicle.
  5. Observe the oxygen meter—it does not decrease, even while underwater.

Expected Behavior:

The oxygen meter should begin to decrease when players are underwater after exiting the Prawn Suit, as these vehicles no longer provide oxygen once the player has exited.

Observed Behavior:

The oxygen meter remains frozen at maximum, granting the player infinite oxygen. This allows for unlimited underwater exploration but breaks immersion and gameplay balance.

Root Cause:

A flag in the oxygen meter system was not being reset correctly when the player exited the Prawn Suit.

Solution:

  • Investigation: Debug logs revealed that the isInsideVehicle boolean remained set to true after exiting the Prawn Suit.
  • Fix Implementation:
    • Added a resetOxygenStatus() function to the vehicle exit event listener.
    • Ensured the isInsideVehicle flag was set to false upon exit, regardless of water depth.
    • Updated shallow water logic to correctly switch oxygen sources between the vehicle and the player.
  • Testing:
    • Reproduced the bug scenario after applying the fix to verify the oxygen meter behaved as expected.
    • Conducted regression testing in both shallow and deep water to ensure no new bugs were introduced.

Resolution:

The bug was fixed in Patch 1.1.25. Players now experience normal oxygen depletion after exiting the Prawn Suit after entering/leaving a portal.

Bug Report Example 2: Missing Terrain Collision

Details:

Title: Seamoth Can Fall Through Terrain

Priority: Medium

Severity: Major

Description:

Seamoth can fall through the terrain, causing it to get stuck under the map. This issue prevents further exploration.
